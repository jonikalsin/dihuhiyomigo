Career Beginnings

Madison’s career started when she was only nine years old, with the title role in the short film “Harmony Parker”, and then a minor role in the film “Conversation with Other Women”. After this, Madison continued with minor roles in the TV series “Numbe3rs” (2005) and “Bones”, and also tried herself as a voice actress, landing the voice role of Quillo in the animated film “Over the Hedge” (2006), with Bruce Willis, Garry Shandling and Steve Carell. Her next role was in the comedy-drama film “Humboldt County” in 2008, with Jeremy Strong and Fairuza Balk in the lead roles, and she also portrayed Ruthie Smithens in the television drama film “Kit Kittredge: An American Girl”. In 2009 she secured her first starring role, as Caroline in the highly-acclaimed independent drama-thriller film “The Attic Door”.

Rise to Prominence

Gradually Madison’s career improved, and she started gaining more notable roles, such as Lindsay Westman in the television family-drama film “Dad’s Home” in 2010, co-starring David James Elliott and Sharon Case, then featuring in the first season of the comedy-drama series “Shameless” (2011-2012), followed by her portrayal of Hannah in the horror film “The Possession”. She continued her rise to stardom with a bunch of successful roles, such as Na’el in the Golden Globe Award-nominated action-adventure film “Noah” in 2014, starring Russell Crowe, Jennifer Connelly and Anthony Hopkins, and then the lead role in the drama film “A Light Beneath Their Feet” (2015), and the part of Haley in the comedy film “Sisters”. Most recently, she portrayed Ashley Wheeler in the TV crime-drama miniseries “Sharp Objects” in 2018, while in 2019 she lent her voice to Nameless Star in the video game “Kingdom Hearts III”.
