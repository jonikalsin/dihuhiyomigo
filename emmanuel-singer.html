What Emmanuel means?

Emmanuel is a masculine name of Hebrew origin. A variant spelling of Immanuel, this name translates to “God is with us,” a wonderful way to introduce baby to your faith. It’s found in the Hebrew Bible and Christian Old Testament and was seen as a sign that God will protect the House of David.

Why is Jesus called Immanuel?

The answer that the biblical story gives is Jesus, Immanuel, God With Us. The reference to this name comes from the prophet Isaiah who first foretold of a virgin or young women conceiving and bearing a child. As the prophet says, this child will be named “Immanuel” (Isa. 7:14).

What does Emmanuel mean in the Bible?

The word Emmanuel appears 4 times in the Bible—three times in Isaiah and once in Matthew. Emmanuel means “God with us” and is a name for Jesus.
