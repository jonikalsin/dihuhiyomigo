Biography of Anthony Bourdain

Anthony Michael Bourdain, a well-known television personality, chef, and author, was born on 25th June 1956 in New York City, USA. He is best known for hosting the travel and food shows “Anthony Bourdain: No Reservations” (2005 – 2012) and “The Layover” (2011 – 2013) on Travel channel, as well as “Anthony Bourdain: Parts Unknown” (2013 – 2018) on CNN. He also authored the bestseller “Kitchen Confidential: Adventures in the Culinary Underbelly” (2000). Unfortunately, he passed away in 2018.

His estimated net worth was $9 million, accumulated primarily through his cooking, writing, and hosting various television shows.

Anthony Bourdain’s Net Worth: $9 Million

Bourdain was raised in Leonia, New Jersey, and received his education at the Dwight-Englewood School. Later, he attended Vassar College before dropping out to study at the Culinary Institute of America. He then worked in numerous restaurants, eventually becoming an executive chef at prestigious restaurants in Manhattan, Washington D.C., Miami, and Tokyo, which significantly contributed to his net worth.

As an author, Bourdain published both fiction and non-fiction books. He gained significant recognition after the success of “Kitchen Confidential: Adventures in the Culinary Underbelly” in 2000. His other popular books, such as “A Cook’s Tour: In Search of the Perfect Meal” (2001) and “Medium Raw: A Bloody Valentine to the World of Food and the People Who Cook” (2010), further bolstered his net worth.

Bourdain’s television programs received numerous nominations and awards, including his show “Anthony Bourdain: Parts Unknown” winning two Emmy Awards for Outstanding Informational Series in 2013 and 2014, as well as a Peabody Award in 2014, all contributing to his wealth.

Bourdain’s unconventional approach to cooking and life established him as a non-traditional personality in the culinary world, leading to his induction into the list of Who’s Who of Food and Beverage in America by the James Beard Foundation in 2008. Additionally, he received an Honorary CLIO Award in 2010 for encouraging people to think differently.

Bourdain was married twice, and he sadly passed away in 2018.
